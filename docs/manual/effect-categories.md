| Category | What it does | Variants | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Distortion** | Adds overdrive, distortion, or fuzz coloration to the signal, ranging from subtle boost through thick saturation to extreme fuzz. | Mono, Stereo, Legacy | Legacy models are mono only. |
| **Dynamics** | Controls signal dynamics via compressors, noise gates, auto-swell, and feedback generation. | Mono, Stereo, Legacy | Feedbacker uses polyphonic pitch shifting — ⚠ DSP (one per preset, up to half a path's DSP). |
| **EQ** | Shapes tone by boosting or cutting frequency bands using parametric, graphic, shelf, or tilt curves. | Mono, Stereo | No Legacy subcategory. |
| **Modulation** | Applies time-varying pitch or amplitude effects: tremolo, vibrato, chorus, flanger, phaser, rotary, ring modulator, and related. | Mono, Stereo, Legacy | Poly Detune uses polyphonic pitch shifting — ⚠ DSP (one per preset, up to half a path's DSP). |
| **Delay** | Repeats the input signal after a set time, from clean digital delays to tape-style, swell, modulated, and multi-tap echoes. | Mono, Stereo, Legacy | Poly Sustain uses polyphonic pitch shifting — ⚠ DSP (one per preset, up to half a path's DSP). |
| **Reverb** | Simulates acoustic spaces (hall, room, plate, spring, shimmer, and others) to add depth and environment to the signal. | Mono, Stereo, Legacy | |
| **Filter** | Sweeps or shapes the frequency spectrum dynamically; includes auto-filters, envelope filters, and wah-pedal models. | Mono, Stereo, Legacy (Filter); Mono, Stereo (Wah) | Wah models appear as a separate subcategory in the model browser alongside Filter. |
| **Pitch/Synth** | Shifts pitch, creates harmonies, generates octave doubling, or synthesizes new tones from the input signal. | Mono, Stereo, Legacy | Poly Pitch, Poly Wham, Poly Capo, and 12 String use polyphonic pitch shifting — ⚠ DSP (one per preset, up to half a path's DSP). |
| **Amp+Cab** | Combines a full amp model with its matched speaker cabinet in a single block, using one of the eight available processing slots. | Guitar, Bass | ⚠ DSP — any combination of Amp+Cab, Amp, or Preamp blocks: max two per preset. |
| **Amp** | Full amplifier model without a cabinet, used when pairing with a separate Cab or IR block. | Guitar, Bass | ⚠ DSP — counts toward the two-amp-block-per-preset limit. |
| **Preamp** | Models only the preamp stage of an amplifier, recommended when feeding into the power-amp return of a real amplifier; requires less DSP than a full Amp block. | Guitar, Bass, Mic | Includes a studio microphone preamp in the Mic subcategory. |
| **Cab** | Speaker cabinet simulation using IR-based models; Single Cab is mono, Dual Cab is stereo with two independent cab and mic selections. | Single, Dual | ⚠ DSP — Dual Cab uses twice the DSP of a Single; combined limit with IR blocks applies (see IR row). |
| **IR** | Loads custom or third-party impulse responses (up to 128 stored on device) to reproduce a specific cab/mic combination or acoustic body resonance. | Single, Dual | ⚠ DSP — up to two Single 1024-point IRs, up to two Single 2048-point IRs, or one Dual IR per preset; limit is shared with Cab and Amp+Cab blocks. |
| **Send/Return** | Routes signal out to external stompboxes or rack gear and returns it at any point in the signal chain, usable as independent sends/returns or as a mono/stereo FX loop. | Send, Return, FX Loop | |
| **Looper** | Records and plays back audio loops of up to 60 seconds (mono) or 30 seconds (stereo) at full speed; the Shuffling Looper adds randomized playback capabilities. | Mono, Stereo | One Looper per preset; must be assigned to a footswitch to function. |
